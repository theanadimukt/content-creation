# Episode 0: Day Zero — I Finally Decided to Start
Date: 2026-06-15
Format: YouTube (~5–6 min) — intro episode, intentionally shorter than main series

---

## Script

> **Production note:** Talking head only. Simple background — your desk or a plain wall. Calm, honest pace. No high energy, no big gestures. Speak like you're talking to one person. No screen recording needed for this episode.

---

**[CAMERA — talking head, looking directly at camera]**

I have been watching other developers on YouTube for years. [pause] Building things, launching products, talking about their journey. I always thought — one day, maybe I'll do that too. [pause] But one day kept moving. So I decided to just start. This is day zero.

**[TEXT OVERLAY — "Day Zero"]**

---

**[SECTION 1: Who I Am]**

**[CAMERA — talking head]**

My name is Sagar. I'm a full-stack developer. I moved from India to the UK in 2021. [pause] I'm not a startup founder. I'm not an entrepreneur. I work a regular job. I have a family. [pause] I'm just a normal developer who wants to build something real.

---

**[SECTION 2: Years of Consuming]**

**[CAMERA — talking head]**

For maybe four or five years now... I have been consuming content. YouTube. Podcasts. Twitter. [pause] I learned a lot. Genuinely. I know how to test if an idea is good. I know how to build a small first version. I know about building in public. [pause] But knowing and doing are very different things. [pause]

I never built anything I was proud of. Every time I had an idea, I would overthink it. Is this the right idea? What if nobody wants it? What if I fail? [pause] And then I would just... not start.

**[TEXT OVERLAY — "Consuming ≠ Building"]**

---

**[SECTION 3: The Decision]**

**[CAMERA — talking head]**

A few weeks ago I had a moment. Nothing dramatic. [pause] I just realised — I am still doing the same thing I was doing four years ago. Still consuming, still waiting, still thinking "maybe next time." [pause]

So I made a decision. I'm going to build something. A small software product. A micro-SaaS. And I'm going to do it in public, with the camera on, documenting every single step.

**[TEXT OVERLAY — "Micro-SaaS. In Public. From Scratch."]**

---

**[SECTION 4: What This Series Is]**

**[CAMERA — talking head]**

Every video in this series is one real step in the journey. [pause] The idea. The validation. The building. The mistakes. I'm going to show all of it. [pause] I'm using AI to help me build faster. But I'm making the decisions. And when something goes wrong — and it will go wrong — I'm going to talk about that too. [pause]

No polishing. No pretending. Just the real thing.

---

**[SECTION 5: Why Build in Public]**

**[CAMERA — talking head]**

Building in public is a bit scary. [pause] When you build in public, you can't hide. If I fail, it's there. If I make a bad decision, it's recorded. [pause] But I think that's good. [pause]

When someone is watching, you try harder. You think more carefully. [pause] Also — I want to find people doing the same thing. People building small products, figuring things out. If this series helps even one person start something... that's enough.

---

**[LESSON]**

**[CAMERA — talking head]**

I used to think I needed to be ready before I started. More experience, better idea, better timing. [pause] But there is no ready. There is only starting.

**[TEXT OVERLAY — "There is no ready. There is only starting."]**

---

**[CTA]**

**[CAMERA — talking head]**

In the next video, I'm going to talk about the idea I'm building. What problem it solves and why I think it's worth building. [pause] If you want to follow this journey, subscribe. And if you're on Instagram, I post shorter updates there too. [pause] See you in the next one.
